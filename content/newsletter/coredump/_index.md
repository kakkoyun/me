CoreDump is a roughly-weekly dump of the problems I actually hit while
working and how I got out of them: the error, the dead end, the fix. A core
dump is the thing you go read to find out what went wrong, which is exactly
what each issue is.

The first draft of every issue is pulled from my coding-session logs by a
tool, then edited by hand before it goes out. No raw machine output lands in
your inbox. It's an experiment in making "write down what you solved" cheap
enough that I actually do it. The story behind that is in
[Write it down. The models are reading.](/posts/write-it-down-the-models-are-reading/)

Past issues sit below and stay readable on the site forever. Subscribe to
get new ones by email, or follow the
[RSS feed](/newsletter/coredump/index.xml).
